Camel

  • Camels are furry
  • Camels can move easily across the sand as their feet are padded so they don't sink
  • if they find water they try to drink as much as possible

Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  • They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.

Chameleon

  • They mainly change their colour in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  • Unlike many lizards they cannot regrow their tails if cut.
  • Almost half of all known species live in madagascar.

Pigeon

  • Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  • Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours.
  • Pigeons have very sensitive sense of hearing. They are able to detect distant storms, earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.

Bear

  • Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts from play to hunting.
  • Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees. They can use these for hiding, eating and even sleeping.
  • Polar bears are actually black not white
